About

I am a scientist. And a leader. An engineer — well, sometimes. Definitely an educator. And a Star Wars fan… can I be a Jedi? Why can't I be all of these things? In fact, I have deliberately refused to choose among them. My research has ranged across materials science, solid mechanics, and computational science, and in recent years has grown to span machine learning, artificial intelligence, and causal reasoning. Across two decades in industry, academia, and government — from General Motors to the Air Force Research Laboratory, a faculty appointment at Mississippi State, and the Army Research Laboratory — the common thread has been modeling in every form — physics-based simulation, machine learning, and data science — understanding mechanisms at one scale to predict behavior at the next.

As a senior leader at DEVCOM ARL, I founded ARL Central, building the laboratory's Midwest presence from a single researcher to 30+ scientists and $40M/year in collaborative research programs. I led the Army Artificial Intelligence Innovation Institute (A2I2) — a collaborative research alliance spanning 116 institutions — through its Phase I capstone and Phase II architecture, and I spent a year of sabbatical at Northwestern and the University of Chicago studying the science of science: how teams, citations, and institutions shape research impact.

I am equally proud of the classroom record: I created and taught "Machine Learning for Everyone: May The Fourth Be With You" — six annual iterations reaching 3,300+ participants from 173+ Army organizations — and co-developed "AI for Soldiers," an in-residence Army HQ Strategic Broadening Seminar bringing AI literacy directly to Soldiers. I hold a PhD in Materials Science and Engineering from Georgia Tech, where my dissertation received the campus-wide Sigma Xi Best PhD Thesis Award.

Research Areas

Atoms → Properties

ICME & Computational Materials Science

Integrated Computational Materials Engineering — atomistic simulation of grain boundaries, dislocation nucleation, and nanocrystalline alloys, linked upward into alloy design and materials discovery.

Mechanisms → Scales

Solid Mechanics & Multiscale Modeling

Deformation, plasticity, and failure across scales — from dislocation mechanics and grain boundary deformation to polymers, ceramics, and the mechanics of engineered microstructures.

Data → Decisions

Machine Learning & Artificial Intelligence

ML applied well beyond materials — manufacturing, information science, and Army decision-making — with current foundational research in causal reasoning and causal inference for AI systems.

Papers → Patterns

Science of Science

Computational social science applied to research itself: bibliometrics, team science, and scientific impact — quantifying how institutions and collaborations shape discovery, and using it to steer R&D strategy.

People → Capability

AI Education & Workforce Development

Designing and teaching AI/ML curricula for scientists, engineers, and Soldiers — intuition-first, spreadsheet-level mechanics that make the math visible, delivered at DoD scale.
